Course content

The 1hp training ‘Open Science in the Swedish context’ is designed to equip scientists within the DDLS research school with a range of tools needed to perform research in accordance with Swedish Open Science standards, in addition to raising awareness around the Open Science and FAIR principles and how to implement these in a researcher’s daily life. The training covers how to implement Open Science across several stages of the research project lifecycle, including experimental planning & design, sharing & disseminating output, researcher evaluation, and science communication. Through interactive lectures, discussions with guest speakers, and practical assignments, participants will gain first-hand experience with implementing and navigating pre-registration, the Open Access publishing landscape, new researcher evaluation criteria, and citizen science. Completing this training will position scientists within the DDLS research school as future leaders in the global shift towards more open and collaborative research practices, and will support Sweden’s commitment to innovation and public engagement, fostering a research environment where knowledge is freely exchanged and built upon.

Learning Objectives and Learning Outcomes

Learning Objectives

This course is designed for participants to:

  • Get acquainted with Open Science principles and how they will be implemented into Swedish academia within the coming years
  • Gain insight in to how to execute every step of a research project according to Open Science principles

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, participants will be able to:

  • Explain what Open Science is, and discuss the benefits and challenges that come with its implementation, specifically in the Swedish context
  • Describe the process for and benefits of publishing a registered report
  • Define the different types of Open Access publishing options and their benefits and limitations
  • Identify new researcher evaluation criteria and demonstrate an understanding of which
    research outputs can be published and credited
  • Explain what citizen science is, and write a popular scientific summary of a scientific project

Target audience and course prerequisites

Target audience: This course is offered to all researchers in the DDLS program.
Course prerequisites: none
Level: beginner/intermediate

Forms of instruction and examination

Forms of instruction: In-person, digital communications
Forms of study: self-studies (incl. independent work), group activities, lectures, and discussion seminars
Language: English
Examination: Active participation in all sessions and group assignments. A written popular scientific communication about a citizen science project of interest

Additional notes

  • This course is highly interactive and hence it is important that you, as a participant, actively contribute to all sessions and elements of the course.
  • Participants need to bring a laptop.
